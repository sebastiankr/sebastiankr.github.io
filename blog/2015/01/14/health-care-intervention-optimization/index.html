Health Care Intervention Optimization

Jan 14th, 2015 11:22 am | Comments

This series of posts details an enterprise architecture (EA) for health care. Feel free to have a look at the other posts in this series:

  1. Enterprise Architecture for Health Care
  2. Health Care Intervention Optimization (this post)

This post focuses on the Intervention Optimization.

Starting with intervention optimization requires us to think about the output of gap identification processes and the input to intervention execution systems. Intervention optimization is at the center and once designed, gap identification and intervention execution will fall into place almost automatically. The following model is the basis for the Intervention Optimization Engine (IOE) application. Try it out here.

The Intervention Optimization Engine receives gaps from downstream applications. Every gap identification system determines the likelihood of a gap existing, the value of a gap, and its cost. These gaps are send to the IOE and it determines which interventions should optimally be executed with a given budget. It then creates a utilization plan by trying to spread out the interventions as evenly as possible across the year. This avoids peaks. An even utilization of intervention processes reduces costs.

The Data Model

But what are gaps and how do they relate to interventions? A logical data model helps to understand the relations and supports a common vocabulary/taxonomy.

A party has associated gaps. A party is either an individual or an organization. In our case it is either a provider or a member.

For our purpose, a gap is something that can be intervened upon. That means that there is a business process that, when executed, can potentially close that gap. An intervention type is exactly one of those business processes. A gap could have multiple possible intervention types associated with it. As an example:

The current demo of the IOE has three types of interventions available. MEOT stands for a member educational outreach via telephone. An educational outreach via mail is a MEOM. IHA is an in-home assessment. There are potentially many more, but it does not matter to the IOE. It is agnostic of details for intervention types. The intervention execution systems know how to conduct these interventions. This is a nice separation of concerns. Intervention execution systems can access the intervention plan of the IOE. They pick interventions form the plan. This does not necessary have to be in order. Intervention execution systems could potentially have their own intelligence build in.

After selection of each intervention, the IOE gets notified and the intervention gets taken out of the optimization and planning process by setting the status from Planned to Started. This can be simulated in the demo application. Interventions have two basic outcomes. Either they are successful and the value for the health plan has been generated or they are unsuccessful and only the cost have been incurred without any benefit for the plan. There are more states for interventions, which can be depicted with a state machine. These states can be used for visualization to provide an overview of the current performance.

Intervention Optimization Engine

Admittedly this model is more targeting health plans, but with minor changes it could equally fit ACO models or data analytic vendors. Please try out the Intervention Optimization Engine. Features are:

  • Full optimization under a second for a plan with up to 500,000 members
    • enables point of care decisions
  • No human involvement necessary because algorithm creates the optimal solution
    • Weights can still be adjusted and interventions be forced (deviates from optimum)
  • Optimization across all Intervention Types
  • Optimization includes probabilities for intervention success
  • Optimization includes probabilities of gap existence
  • Can include all intervention types
  • Advanced reporting
  • Easy integration of new gaps and interventions
  • Accounting for not conducting interventions

Try out the Intervention Optimization Engine and let me know if you have any questions.
